"We are committed to bringing quality employment services to Southeastern CT because we envision all people working and living healthy, fulfilled lives."

For Employers

Southeastern Employment Services (SES) has been helping area employers with their staffing needs for more than a decade. All of our employment services are provided at no charge to the business. We work in partnership with you and tailor our sevices to meet your needs. We will match job seekers to suitable jobs, will help in training the new employee, will offer support to the employee as well as the employer and continually work as a liaison to help problem solve any job related issue, saving you time and money.

Assessment / Work Evaluations

SES provides "pre-screened" individuals to meet the needs of local businesses. There is no cost for an employer to participate in this program and it is typically completed in one to two weeks. Connecticut state agencies provide funding for these employment services. Benefits to the individual include:

  • Pre-screened applicants who know the needs of your business
  • An SES Job Coach will be with the individual to assist them to learn the skills needed to complete tasks and become a valued part of the work environment
  • Both the prospective employee and the Job Coach are on the SES payroll during this work evaluation
  • A certificate of insurance will be provided to your business
  • You are under no obligation to hire this individual
  • You will help an individual to learn about the job skills needed for success in this type of work

School to Work Transition Program

School to Work Transition Program provides an "employment internship" with local businesses to high school students. These internships give the students an opportunity to experience job requirements and environments first hand while providing the business with potential employees familiar with the job requirements. Internships are typically 20 hours per week. Benefits to the employer include:

  • Potential employees who know the skills needed to complete work tasks in your business, and the expectations of your work environment
  • An SES Job Coach will be with the individual to assist them to learn skills needed to complete tasks, and become a valued part of the work environment.
  • Both the prospective employee and the Job Coach are on the payroll of SES during this work evaluation
  • A certificate of insurance will be provided to your business
  • There is no placement cost to an employer for the employee

Working Interviews

Working Interviews allow an employer to observe a potential employee for a period of one to two weeks before making the decision to hire. Employers have the chance to get to know the potential employee and observe their skills and contribution to the work place. Working Interviews typically occur for one to two weeks; employers then make a hiring decision after getting to know the person searching for a job. Benefits to the business include:

  • Save the time and money associated with advertising and interviewing for job vacancies Pre-screened applicants who know the needs of your business and with whom you and your staff have developed a relationship
  • An SES job coach will be with the individual to assist them to learn skills needed to complete tasks, and become a valued part of the work environment
  • Both the prospective employee and the job coach are on the payroll of SES during this work evaluation
  • A certificate of insurance will be faxed or e-mailed to your business
  • When an individual is hired, SES continues to provide support and follow-up services as needed

On the Job Training / Paid Internships

On the Job Training and Paid Internships provide an opportunity for employers to be reimbursed for training a new employee that can meet your specific needs before making a permanent job offer. While in the program the employer will work with a State of CT Bureau of Rehabilitation Services (BRS) representative to develop a training plan and provide assistance with recommendations and resources. BRS will pay for the employer's training expense and ensure that the new employee has supports, if needed.
